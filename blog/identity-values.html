Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why

I was in a multidisciplinary team of engineers and management. We were in the initial stage of the project where the product was being scoped out. This is just a fancy way of saying that what was required of the product was airy. However we had to decide on a specific module that was needed to run this product. There was a bit of tension between the firmware engineer, lead engineer and management. The tension came from lack of understanding of firmware and also the airy product requirements. I took it upon myself to truly understand what the firmware engineer wanted in laymen terms and relayed that information to the rest of the team formally and informally. This meant that everyone had all the information when making the decision on what modules to use. The strategies I used here was not to demonise the firmware engineer but to understand the reasons behind the reasoning. Kind of separate the individual from the task and when we discussed it, we spent time looking at the facts and requirements and how we could meet them. It proved very helpful in moving forward. If I had to do it again, I would of challenged the requirements a bit more to understand what was high priority and what was low priority. I think this would of better aligned the team to what was important when making large decisions.
