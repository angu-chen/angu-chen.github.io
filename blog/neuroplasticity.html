Chat about Neuroplaticity and Growth Mindset

In this blog post, we will have a chat about Neurosplasticity and the growth mindset.

Neuroplaticity

Neurosplasticity is the brains ability to grow, change and adapt throughout a persons life. Extrapolated, this means that humans have the ability to improve their skills and recover from trauma in an understandable way. This is very powerful as it gives individuals the ability to transform their own life.

“Neurons that are used frequently develop stronger connections. Those that are rarely or never used eventually die. By developing new connections and pruning away weak ones, the brain can adapt to the changing environment.” - verywellmind

One of the key principles of neuroplasticity is when a neuron pathway is fire, the pathway becomes the insulation(myelin sheath) grows thicker. This in turn improves the ease of the neuron pathway to fire. In laymen terms, it is about the frequency of practice vs the cramming of practice that will improve your brains ability to develop.

Another great idea is that the skills you have now are not always going to be the only set of skills you will ever have. A neuroplastic brain means anyone is capable of learning new skills and improve on the said skills. Lastly you can make use of the physiology of the brain in order to enhance the speed in which you learn. An example is to exercise regularly. Regular aerobic exercise creates more blood flow into the brain and enhances the availability of Brain Derived Neurotropic Factors (BDNF) which are involved in cognitive functions.

Another example is the practice of mindfulness and how meditation can increase cortical thickness in areas related to attention and sensory processing.

Are there ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Yes, there definitely is. However they are not particularly “sexy” as one might think. It includes basic life admin and living a healthy life. Below is a list of areas which will effect the brains neuroplastic nature.

  • Physcial Exercise
  • Good sleep
  • healthy diet
  • learning new things
  • consistently challenging yourself
  • meditation and reflection
