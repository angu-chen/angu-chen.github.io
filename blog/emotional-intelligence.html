Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EQ)is the ability to understand and process your own and others feelings and actions. There is five elements to EQ that form the core building blocks.

  1. Self-Awareness- understanding your own emotions and the actions that come from them
  2. Self-Regulation - The ability to process, monitor and regulate your actions, emotions and thoughts depending on the situation
  3. Motivation- The ability to motivate yourself based on the idea of deferring short-term satisfaction for long-term sucess
  4. Empathy- The ability to understand how others are feeling and why they are feeling such emotions. It is also the ability to accept it even if you don't completely get it.
  5. Social Skills- The ability to build rapport and trust with others.

All of these elements are interrelated and therefore some of the skills are needed in one or more core building blocks. An example is in order to have great social skills and the ablity to generate rapport, it is important to be empathetic.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ is unlike IQ because it is constantly developing and you have the ability to improve it. IQ is normally set. IQ is more concerned about your ability to solve problems while EQ is about how you interact and see yourself in the world.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it is essential to working well in teams, being a good leader and many other human-human interactions. Human- human interactions also extends to the metaphorical, i.e. you interacting with yourself.
